# Users and roles

Updated: 2026-07-05

Wexio is built for different user types. A company workspace should not expose the same screens, tools, or actions to everyone.

## Main role idea

Wexio separates two concepts:

1. the human or business role of the user;
2. the functional access profile needed for specific work.

This makes it possible to keep the workspace understandable while still supporting finance, sales, support, HR, admin, worker, client, and supplier-oriented use cases.

## Public role groups

| Role group | Public explanation |
|---|---|
| SuperAdmin | Highest-level owner or platform-level control where explicitly allowed. |
| Admin | Company workspace administration and configuration. |
| Manager | Operational management and coordination. |
| Employee | Internal daily workspace user. |
| Worker | Focused worker screen, not necessarily the full internal desktop. |
| Client | Limited customer-facing access where enabled. |
| Supplier | Limited supplier-facing access where enabled. |
| Guest | Restricted view or portal-style access. |

## Functional profiles

Functional profiles may be layered on top of a user role. Examples include finance, accounting, sales, support, HR, or other module-specific access bundles.

## Practical pilot questions

Before a pilot, define:

- who needs full workspace access;
- who needs limited access;
- who can manage users;
- who can manage documents;
- who can view finance or HR information;
- which users should only use a focused surface.

## Important rule

A role label is not enough by itself. Access depends on the configured workspace, enabled modules, permissions, and the selected pilot scope.
