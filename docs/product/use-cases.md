# Public use cases

Updated: 2026-07-04

This page gives examples of where Wexio can be evaluated during a controlled pilot.

## Use case 1: Company workspace

A company wants one place where users can access modules, documents, widgets, and business records.

Wexio value to test:

- clearer navigation;
- fewer disconnected tools;
- personal desktop for each internal user;
- controlled access by role.

## Use case 2: Documents and client follow-up

A company has documents stored in different locations and customer follow-up spread across messages, folders, and spreadsheets.

Wexio value to test:

- documents connected to business context;
- clearer client records;
- explicit sharing;
- easier search and review.

## Use case 3: Time tracking and HR basics

A company wants to understand work time, employees, contracts, leave, or workforce records in a more structured way.

Wexio value to test:

- time tracking as part of the workspace;
- HR records linked to users;
- clearer operational follow-up;
- less manual collection.

## Use case 4: Finance and invoicing preparation

A company wants better visibility around invoicing, expenses, reporting, and business control.

Wexio value to test:

- invoice preparation inside the workspace;
- finance surfaces connected to client and product records;
- reporting visibility;
- separation between internal workflow and external provider steps.

## Use case 5: Projects and workflow

A company wants to track tasks, responsibilities, deadlines, and operational stages.

Wexio value to test:

- workflow visibility;
- fewer missed follow-ups;
- clearer ownership;
- better overview for managers.

## Use case 6: Operational expansion

After a first pilot, a company may evaluate products, inventory, logistics, support, analytics, or other operational modules.

The correct approach is step-by-step expansion, not activating every module at once.
