# Detailed module landscape

Updated: 2026-07-04

This page explains the Wexio module families in business language. It is not a promise that every company receives every module. Availability depends on the selected pilot scope, workspace setup, role, configuration, and rollout status.

## Catalogue language

Wexio separates product surfaces into clear categories.

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Module | A business capability enabled for a company workspace. |
| App | A full screen or window used inside a module. |
| Tool | A utility such as settings, file navigation, or workspace control. |
| Widget | A small desktop card for visibility or quick access. |
| Shortcut | A link to a module, tool, app, or website. |
| Provider app | A Wexio administration surface, separate from a normal company workspace. |
| Unavailable module | A module that exists in the platform but is not enabled, allowed, ready, or configured for the current workspace. |

## Workspace and control

### Users and access

Used to manage people, memberships, roles, and workspace visibility. This is the foundation for who can see what.

### Desktop workspace

The desktop workspace gives internal users a personal working area with layout, widgets, shortcuts, and active module entry points.

### Workflow

Workflow supports movement across business processes: stages, tasks, notifications, and operational follow-up.

### Projects and tasks

Projects and tasks help companies track delivery, responsibilities, deadlines, and internal coordination.

## Commercial areas

### CRM and clients

CRM and client management help centralize prospects, customers, companies, contacts, statuses, and follow-up.

### Sales and offers

Sales and offers support the commercial flow before invoicing or delivery.

### Products

Products are shared business objects used by sales, inventory, invoicing, and operational flows.

### Marketing

Marketing supports public communication, lead capture, and business visibility where enabled.

## Operational execution

### Documents

Documents cover files, categorization, preview, sharing, and workspace organization.

### Support

Support can manage tickets, customer questions, internal handling, and follow-up.

### Assets and organization

Assets and organization tools help structure company resources and internal operating context.

## People and workforce

### HR

HR can support employees, contracts, leave, approvals, and workforce records depending on rollout scope.

### Time tracking

Time tracking helps teams record work time, timesheets, and workforce activity where enabled.

### Worker and client surfaces

Worker and client users can receive dedicated limited screens instead of the full internal desktop.

## Finance and control

### Finance

Finance can cover expenses, transactions, bank accounts, reporting, VAT-oriented information, and accounting support surfaces depending on validation state.

### Invoicing

Invoicing is a focused billing and invoice workflow surface.

### Peppol

Peppol is treated as an internal Wexio module in the current pilot focus. External provider delivery remains a separate configuration and proof step.

### Analytics and reports

Analytics and reports turn workspace data into operational visibility.

### Compliance

Compliance supports the control and auditability direction of the platform.

## Supply chain and production

Wexio also maps wider business areas such as inventory, warehouse, purchasing, logistics, manufacturing, production, and quality. These areas are part of the broader platform direction and should be activated only when useful and validated.

## Provider operations

Provider operations are Wexio-side administration areas. They are not normal tenant work screens and must stay separated from company user workspaces.

## Key rule

A module existing in the Wexio platform does not automatically mean it is active, ready, or included for every pilot. Pilot value comes from selecting the right modules for the company's real problem.
